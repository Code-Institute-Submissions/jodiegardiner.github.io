The Pullman is hip, modern boutique hotel. They've filmed recent Korean dramas like "Boys Before Flowers" and "Style" here, per the information in the lobby. It's sleek, yet comfortable, and I loved our stay there. The only issue for some may be the bathroom set up. As you can see from one of the pictures, all that separates the bathroom from the bed area is a glass wall. You have the option of pulling a screen down for "privacy," but you can still see silhouettes through the screen. In addition, the other side of the bathroom where the door is, has a small gap in the door. So if you have any qualms about sharing your bathroom habits with your travel mate, such as a co-worker, this may not be the best place for you. In addition, the rise of the bathtub wall may be difficult for older or shorter people to manage. I was traveling with a group, and the older people all didn't like the hotel because they had a hard time getting in/out of the tub. There are no grab bars to hold onto. I'm 5'10" and had difficulty getting in, so it may be a challenge for someone shorter. Also in the middle of the night, around 3 am, I was awakened by trucks outside. Perhaps they were trash trucks? It was extremely loud for about 10 minutes, so that really affected my sleep. I was on a lower floor (the 7th) -- my friends who were higher up (I think the 21st?) didn't hear a thing.
